About His Excellency

Learn about President Yoweri Kaguta Museveni's leadership journey and achievements

A Legacy of Transformational Leadership

Yoweri Kaguta Museveni was born in 1944 in Rwakitura, Uganda. He attended Ntare School, and later university in Dar es Salaam, Tanzania where he studied political science and economics.

President Museveni led the National Resistance Movement (NRM) that ended years of political turmoil in 1986, becoming President of Uganda. Since then, he has overseen Uganda's transformation into a more stable, democratic, and economically prosperous nation.

With decades of leadership experience, President Museveni continues to champion pan-African unity, economic development, and regional security cooperation.

For the 2026 elections, he seeks another term to consolidate the gains made and complete ongoing developmental projects across Uganda.

Key Achievements

Highlights from President Museveni's tenure in office

Economic Growth

Uganda's GDP has grown consistently, with significant expansion in agriculture, services, and emerging industries.

Infrastructure Development

Massive investments in roads, railways, and energy projects have transformed Uganda's landscape.

Peace & Security

Restored stability across most regions and professionalized Uganda's security forces.

Education For All

Universal primary and secondary education programs have dramatically increased literacy rates.

Clean Water Access

Expanded rural water coverage benefiting millions of Ugandans previously without clean water.

Healthcare Improvement

Built hospitals and health centers nationwide, improving healthcare accessibility.
